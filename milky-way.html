01 — Our Galaxy

Our Home Galaxy

The Milky Way is a huge collection of stars, dust and gas. It’s called a spiral galaxy because if you could view it from the top or bottom, it would look like a spinning pinwheel.

The Sun is located on one of the spiral arms, about 25,000 light-years away from the center of the galaxy. Even if you could travel at the speed of light (300,000 kilometers, or 186,000 miles, per second), it would take you about 25,000 years to reach the middle of the Milky Way.

02 — Name Origin

Name Origin

The Milky Way gets its name from a Greek myth about the goddess Hera who sprayed milk across the sky. In other parts of the world, our galaxy goes by other names.

In China it’s called the “Silver River,” and in the Kalahari Desert in Southern Africa, it’s called the “Backbone of Night.”

Milky Way Fun Facts

Know 6 Fun Facts About Our Galaxy

01
Warped

The Milky Way galaxy is an enormous, disk-like galaxy that consists of a central bulge and spans a distance of roughly 120,000 light years in diameter. It does not have a perfect shape but is actually twisted and warped due to the gravitational influence of neighboring galaxies such as the Magellanic Clouds.

02
Invisible Halo

Our galaxy is enclosed in an enormous halo of dark matter, which constitutes about 90 percent of the mass of our galaxy. This halo exerts enough gravitational pull on stars that without it, stars would escape into space due to their velocity. The halo cannot be seen with the naked eye, but its presence can be scientifically proven through observing its gravitational effect on the stars’ fast-moving orbits.

03
Over 200 Billion Stars

The Milky Way Galaxy is a relatively average-sized galaxy, with more than 200 billion stars existing within it, including both small red dwarfs and huge blue stars such as our own Sun. Though this sounds impressive, it is not even close to matching the numbers of the biggest galaxy in the universe, which is IC 1101 and includes more than 100 trillion stars.

04
Formed From other Galaxies

The Milky Way did not emerge alone; it has grown through the process of amalgamating with other galaxies for billions of years. As a result of absorption, stars from a small galaxy join the Milky Way galaxy. Presently, the Milky Way is absorbing the Canis Major Dwarf Galaxy and incorporating its stars into the Milky Way's spiraling arms. This is a natural occurrence in the universe that assists in the growth and evolution of the Milky Way galaxy.

05
Constantly Moving

The Milky Way Galaxy is not fixed in place but moves at an astonishingly fast pace through the universe. Along with other galaxies that make up the Local Group, it moves at a speed of 600 kilometers per second (2.2 million kilometers per hour). The velocity of the galaxy is determined using the Cosmic Microwave Background, which is the leftover radiation from the big bang.

06
Almost as old as the universe

The Milky Way itself is around 13.6 billion years old and started to form only 100 million years after the beginning of time known as the Big Bang. Whereas the Milky Way bulge and the Milky Way Halo came first in formation, the spiral arms of our galaxy were formed much later in time.
