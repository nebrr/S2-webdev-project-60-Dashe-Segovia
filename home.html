What is a planet?

A planet is a celestial body that orbits a star, is massive enough to be rounded by its own gravity (hydrostatic equilibrium), and has cleared its neighboring region of other objects.

This definition was adopted by the International Astronomical Union (IAU) in 2006.

Our Solar System officially has 8 planets: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune.
