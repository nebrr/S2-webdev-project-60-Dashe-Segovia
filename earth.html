01 - Our Home

Our Home Planet

Earth, our home planet, is a world unlike any other. The third planet from the sun, Earth is the only place in the known universe confirmed to host life.

With a radius of 3,959 miles, Earth is the fifth largest planet in our solar system, and it's the only one known for sure to have liquid water on its surface.

Every other solar system planet was named for a Greek or Roman deity, but for at least a thousand years some cultures have called our world by the germanic word "earth" - meaning simply "the ground" or "the soil".

02 - Atmosphere

A Shield around Our World

Earth's atmosphere is 78% nitrogen and 21% oxygen — a precise mix that burns incoming meteors, blocks lethal ultraviolet radiation, and traps just enough solar heat to keep the surface liquid rather than frozen.

The ozone layer, the magnetosphere generated by Earth's rotating iron core, and the water cycle all work simultaneously to keep surface temperatures within the narrow band life requires.

Why Earth Must
Be Protected

Six forces shaping the future of the only home we have ever known.

01
Climate Change

Rising CO₂ is shifting temperature bands, disrupting rainfall patterns, and accelerating ice melt at both poles on timescales of centuries.

02
Biodiversity

The current rate of species extinction is estimated at 100 to 1,000 times the natural background rate. Every species lost is a thread pulled from the web that makes agriculture, clean water, and breathable air possible.

03
The Oceans

Oceans absorb about 30% of the CO₂ humans produce. Acidification is already dissolving the shells of marine organisms at the base of the food chain, threatening ecosystems that feed over three billion people.

04
Fresh Water

Only 2.5% of Earth's water is fresh, and most of that is locked in glaciers. Aquifers supplying drinking water to billions are being drawn down faster than rainfall can replenish them.

05
The Atmosphere

The ozone layer filters ultraviolet radiation that would otherwise make surface life impossible. Maintaining atmospheric chemistry is not optional — it is the precondition for everything else on this planet.

06
Geoligical Time

Earth has survived five mass extinctions. Each time, life recovered — but recovery took tens of millions of years. The planet will outlast us. The question is whether conditions for human civilisation will too.
